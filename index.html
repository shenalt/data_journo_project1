Welcome to the United States of Corporate America

The conclusion of the 2024 federal election cycle has been part of a decade-long trend which saw north of $4 billion in outside contributions injected into candidates' campaigns, a record-breaking amount.

By: Shenal Tissera
November 24, 2024

Money has infiltrated our election system.

This past cycle saw the most outside spending going into American elections of all time. $4.4 billion to be exact and has been part of a deeply concerning trend for the past fifteen years.

When it was all said and done, Democrats received $2.6 billion in outside spending money and Republicans received $1.8 billion. Kamala Harris accounted for more than half of the spending from outside groups for Democrats, having north of $1.3 billion to play around with which more than tripled the amount of money Donald Trump received from outside groups.

More than half of the eye-watering four billion dollars of total outside spending came from sources that had either zero or partial disclosure.

Ever since the decisions of the Supreme Court cases Citizens United v. Federal Election Commission and SpeechNow v. Federal Election Commission in 2010, outside spending in American elections has exponentially increased. After more than a decade, it seems the decision to lift limits on corporate contributions towards elections had the shocking effect of releasing an endless amount of money into the election system.

More broadly, Super PACs and the wealthiest individual donors heavily skewed conservative in terms of their contributions to candidates on the federal level.
